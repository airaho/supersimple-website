Playing Volleyball

Serving

What it is: The technique used to put the ball into play from behind the baseline. It’s the first action of every rally.

Types: Underhand, overhand, jump serve, and float serve.

Purpose: Aimed at placing the ball in difficult spots for the opponent to return.

Passing

What it is: The act of receiving the serve or an opponent’s attack using the forearms.

Purpose: Directs the ball to the setter to initiate an offensive play.

Setting

What it is: The second contact where the setter positions the ball near the net for an attacker.

Purpose: Creates an opportunity for hitters to spike the ball effectively.

Spiking

What it is: A forceful, downward strike of the ball aimed at scoring a point.

Purpose: The main offensive move designed to win points by hitting the ball into the opponent’s court.

Blocking

What it is: A defensive move at the net to stop or deflect the opponent’s spike.

Purpose: Prevents the ball from crossing into your court or slows it down to allow teammates to make a play.

Diving

What it is: Techniques used to reach low balls quickly; rolling is a controlled movement, while diving involves fully extending the body.

Purpose: To extend defensive plays by retrieving balls that are out of reach with regular digs.

These skills form the foundation of volleyball, requiring practice and coordination to execute effectively in gameplay.
